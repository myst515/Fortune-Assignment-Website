Overview

Our group selected Cash and Cash Equivalents from The Coca-Cola Company's financial statements as the central focus of our project. This choice aligns with the purpose of general-purpose financial reporting, as outlined in the Conceptual Framework for Financial Reporting: to provide financial information that is useful to existing and potential investors, lenders, and other creditors for making decisions.

The cash position of a company is a critical indicator of its liquidity, financial flexibility, and short-term solvency. This line item allows users to evaluate Coca-Cola‚Äôs ability to:

By understanding the company‚Äôs cash and equivalents, users are better equipped to assess its capacity to generate value, reduce risk, and adapt to market fluctuations. This embodies the framework‚Äôs core principle of decision-usefulness.

Our analysis also prioritizes the qualitative characteristic of faithful representation. Insights

As of December 31, 2024, The Coca-Cola Company reported $1.14 billion in cash and cash equivalents. This balance primarily comprises investments in money market funds, time deposits, and commercial paper with maturities of 90 days or less.

Glossary

  • Cash Equivalents: Short-term, highly liquid investments
  • Liquidity: The ability to meet short-term obligations
  • Quick Ratio: (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities
  • Operating Activities: Cash flows from core operations
  • Free Cash Flow: Cash left after capital expenditures
  • Repatriation Tax: Tax on bringing foreign earnings home
